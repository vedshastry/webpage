About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at the University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC). My research interests are in economic history and development economics -- particularly in long-term climate shifts, adaptation and structural transformation.

Prior to grad school, I contributed to several projects in academic research and public policy at the World Bank (DIME/DECDI) and Indian School of Business (ISB).

I'm enthusiastic about technology, travelling, playing music, chess, learning languages and cooking a meal every Sunday. I also maintain some forks of open-source software that I find useful on GitHub.

Work in Progress

  • Night and Day: Temperature and Structural Transformation in India
    (Draft available on request)
    Do rising temperatures accelerate or arrest structural transformation? Using data from the Indian Census (1981-2011), I find a heterogeneous mechanism between persistent daytime vs nighttime heat on agricultural land and labor. Over 10 years, rising nighttime dew points Δ(Twmin) improve yields and lower prices of grains, while higher daytime wet-bulb temperatures (ΔTwmax) damage cultivable land and depress agricultural wages. Higher ΔTwmin shifts the labor composition from seasonal to full-time agriculture, while ΔTwmax reallocates distressed agricultural labor into informal urban services. Over 30 years, warming nights fuel nonagricultural demand, while peak daytimes arrest structural transformation.
