About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at the University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC). My research studies agricultural transformation, climate change and economic development. I am currently interested in the impacts of rising temperatures and the role of connectivity in the rural economy.

I have previously contributed to research with the World Bank's Development Impact Group (DIME/DECDI). Prior to that, I assisted projects in economics and public policy at the Indian School of Business (ISB).

Beyond academia, I am enthusiastic about computers and technology. I enjoy playing chess, table tennis tournaments, the guitar, singing and occasionally songwriting. I also maintain a few repos and tools that I find useful to my workflow on Github.
