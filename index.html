About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at the University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC). My research interests are in economic history and development economics -- particularly in long-term climate shifts, adaptation and structural transformation.

Prior to grad school, I contributed to several projects in academic research and public policy at the World Bank (DIME/DECDI) and Indian School of Business (ISB).

Work in Progress

  • Night and Day: Warming Temperatures and Structural Transformation in India
    [Paper] [Online Appendix]
    This paper finds diverging partial effects of diurnal warming -- higher nighttime (Tmin) and daytime (Tmax) temperatures -- on the share of agricultural labor in the Indian Census (1981--2011). Both warmer nights and days shrink grain production and land-use. Only warmer days raise grain harvest prices locally. Warmer nights consolidate agriculture, shifting seasonal workers and self-cultivators into wage labor. Warmer days casualize employment, pushing agricultural labor to seasonal work. Long-differenced estimates show the night-day divergence is strong in rural areas. In towns, both warming measures depress nonagricultural worker shares.
